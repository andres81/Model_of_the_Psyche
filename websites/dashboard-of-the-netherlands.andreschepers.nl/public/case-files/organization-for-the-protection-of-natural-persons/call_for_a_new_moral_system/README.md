## Introduction

Looking at the increase in failure rate of the Dutch legal system to provide
justice for victims, it is time to evaluate the functional architecture of
Dutch society. At first glance, it seems that the legal system is an old
system that worked in the past when it comes to providing justice. However,
as a judge goes over all the facts, philosophizes regarding the moral of the
law and the situation, the intentions of all involved, to then decide how
the law applies, it seems necessary to have an institute in place that acts
like a judge in any given situation whether the legal system looks at it or
not, to go to the fullest extent possible, deciding who was wrong, is the
situation acceptable and what the intervention should be.

In other words, the legal system does
not guarantee justice in all situations
to the fullest extent. On top of that, it also does not guarantee that
society learns and gives a signal that certain behaviors will not be
tolerated: The risk you take not dealing with this shortcoming of this moral
system that is the legal system of The Netherlands is that people
rationalize that asking forgiveness is easier than asking permission. Also,
playing the victim right after things go wrong might become a useful tool
to plan cheaper ways of doing things while taking the risk that it will go
wrong, but getting away with it because due to the nature of how the social
contract works, it has become seemingly "accepted".

## The role of the judge

Take, for example, the water cannon incident in Eindhoven in January 2022. The
judge ruled that the police officer should not be prosecuted because the
violence used by the police officer using the water cannon was according to
the
"[ambtsinstructie](https://wetten.overheid.nl/jci1.3:c:BWBR0006589&hoofdstuk=2&paragraaf=3&artikel=14&z=2026-01-01&g=2026-01-01)"
that refers to
[Artikel 26: Mobiele eenheid](https://wetten.overheid.nl/jci1.3:c:BWBR0036705&hoofdstuk=5&artikel=26&z=2025-04-01&g=2025-04-01).

Whether that is correct or not (the judge might be corrupt in an imaginable
nightmare fuel situation), assuming the judge did his job and that his ruling is
correct, the result is that society does not scrutinize the violence itself:
Society delegates the moral obligation of scrutinizing the used
violence by society against a natural person to the legal system, that in
the case of Eindhoven said "No, we shall not."

But as described on Wikipedia:
[Even though we delegate responsibility to moral systems, we always remain
responsible in the end.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bad_faith_(existentialism)#Freedom_and_morality)
That means here that the moral obligation to scrutinize the violence used,
meaning as much as: Do we wish to allow this type of violence, shooting with
the water cannon at two persons from a short distance, with a wall right
behind them, with the risk of killing them, which almost happened, with the
most powerful water beam possible with a water cannon, comes back to society,
meaning the citizens of The Netherlands. Citizens then walk into the wall of
needing to take responsibility of getting justice for the victim of the water
beam violence. But society did not walk into a wall: Justice for the woman in
Eindhoven is
only for her, and it seems not so much of a problem for society that she does
not get it, because who in The Netherlands went to the
[Malieveld](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malieveld)
to protest 
for her justice, and in turn the justice for all of us citizens?

And then to make matters worse: The gaslighting of society initiated by police 
unions and one officer of the
law,
[Xander Simonis](https://panorama.nl/artikel/484494/agent-die-met-waterkanon-in-gezicht-van-denisa-spoot-tijdens-avondklokrellen-wordt-vervolgd)
in the form of
saying that she shouldn't have been there, and concluding on behalf of
society that it was appropriate violence in general, hoping society will be
lazy and gladly accept the offer of having the responsibility delegated to
another moral system: The police force itself, which would be then a case of
["Wij van Wc-eend adviseren Wc-eend"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toilet_Duck#Advertising_slogan).
"We have a monopoly on violence!", maybe hoping citizens take it as a easy 
way to rationalize the misconception of treating that agreement between the 
police and society, with "having a monopoly on deciding what violence is 
allowed to be used in the context of the 'ambtsinstructie'".

And if, we the citizens, don't protest this, let this happen, this way of 
achieving justice or allowing justice not to be served, by the rules of the 
[social contract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_contract)
in a society,
[it will become the law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silence_procedure).
